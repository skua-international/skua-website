BREAKING — EXPLOSION AND CHEMICAL ATTACK IN CENTRAL LONDON
Deadly Explosion Followed by Chemical Release Rocks London; International Tensions Escalate
By AAN Security Desk • Updated 14 minutes ago • London

London, UK — A coordinated attack involving an explosion followed by the release of a violent chemical agent has left central London in lockdown, with emergency services responding to what officials describe as one of the most serious domestic security incidents in years.

The attack occurred near Westminster during the morning rush, when a powerful blast tore through a crowded pedestrian area before a dense vapor cloud spread through surrounding streets. Witnesses described scenes of panic as civilians fled the explosion site, only for many to collapse moments later.

Emergency responders arriving on scene reported multiple individuals suffering acute respiratory distress, convulsions, and loss of consciousness. Dozens were treated at the scene, with several transported to hospitals in critical condition. Authorities warned that casualty numbers may rise as exposure assessments continue.

Police believe the initial explosion may have been used to disperse the chemical agent, a tactic designed to maximize exposure and complicate evacuation. Hazmat units established mass decontamination corridors, while armed officers expanded the security perimeter across multiple blocks. Government buildings across central London were placed under heightened protection.

Investigators recovered equipment fragments and discarded digital media at the scene bearing insignia associated with security contractors operating under the Canton Protocol Strategic Alliance Treaty (CSAT). Senior officials, speaking on condition of anonymity, described the materials as "highly indicative." The Metropolitan Police cautioned that the investigation remains in its earliest stages.

Officials from the People's Republic of China, a leading CSAT member, issued a rapid denial, rejecting any involvement and warning against "dangerous speculation." Diplomatic channels between London and CSAT capitals were reportedly activated within hours of the incident.

The Director General of MI5 released a statement confirming that intelligence agencies are coordinating with domestic and international partners and that all lines of inquiry remain open. AAN understands that forensic teams are working to confirm the chemical agent's precursor signature, a process expected to take several days.

The incident comes amid heightened geopolitical tension following the recent uprising in the Horizon Islands, formerly under Chinese occupation. The unrest resulted in CSAT-aligned forces losing control of the Tanoa Archipelago, a critical Pacific trade route. Analysts say the loss has significantly weakened CSAT strategic positioning in the region, raising fears that retaliatory or destabilizing actions could follow.

Security experts warn that the combination of a high-casualty chemical attack in a major European capital and the current geopolitical climate risks rapid escalation, with several Western governments already signalling a willingness to consider coordinated response measures.

Armed patrols have increased across central London, with additional protective measures implemented around transport hubs, diplomatic missions, and government facilities. Officials described the situation as highly sensitive and warned that developments could have international repercussions.
